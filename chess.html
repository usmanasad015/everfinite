How to Play Chess: A Beginner’s Guide to Learn Chess at Home




Chess was first originated in India around the 6th century by a person named Chaturanga. It is one of the most popular indoor games for two players.

It consists of a chessboard and six unique pieces, includes King, Queen, Rook, Bishop, Knight, and Pawn. Each player has 16 of them, including 8 Pawns, 2 Rooks, 2 Knights, 2 Bishops, 1 Queen, and 1 King.

In this guide, you will learn about how to play chess following all the rules properly.

How to Play Chess

Before starting a game, you should know basic chess rules. You should know the proper chessboard layout, pieces roles with their moves, and situations to win or draw a game.

Place the Chess Board Correctly

A chessboard should be arranged in a proper way to enjoy perfect gameplay. But you may don’t know how to set up a chess board.

A correct way to do so is by placing white squares on the right. Such that, for each player, the light square should be at the bottom right corner.

After placing the chessboard, place all pawns at the second rank on the board. Then place Rooks at both corners. Place knights next to the rooks and bishops as neighbors on both sides. The two squares left are for Queen and King, such that a queen goes on the same color, and the king occupies the only place left behind.

Start with White

The chessboard square and pieces have two colors, light and dark (generally white and black). The white always moves first, and then the other takes the turn. Then the game proceeds according to the rules.

Move Pieces According to the Rules

Pawn

A pawn can move just one square. However, it can move either one or two squares on the very first move. It can only capture the piece in the diagonal and cannot move backward.





Pawn has a special ability to get promoted when it reaches 8th rank (line). You can promote it to any piece you want. You can even get up to nine queens or eight any desired pieces.



Rook

Rook follows a + sign pattern in a move. It can move any number of squares in forwards, backward, left, and right directions. It can not move diagonally.





Knight

Knight follows an L shape pattern in a move. It can move two squares in one direction and then one towards left or right in a single move.

Rook follows a + sign pattern in a move. It can move any number of squares in forwards, backward, left, and right directions. It can not move diagonally.





Bishop

Bishop can only move diagonally. However, it can move any number of squares on the board while no other piece is blocking the way.

Rook follows a + sign pattern in a move. It can move any number of squares in forwards, backward, left, and right directions. It can not move diagonally.





Queen

Queen can move anywhere following a combined pattern of all other pieces except the knight. She can move any number of squares forwards, backward, sideways, and diagonally.

Rook follows a + sign pattern in a move. It can move any number of squares in forwards, backward, left, and right directions. It can not move diagonally.





King

King can move in all directions but only one square in a single move. However, it cannot move in any of the surrounding squares of the opponent king. Because, as per rule, the king can not move to a square where it can get checked.

Rook follows a + sign pattern in a move. It can move any number of squares in forwards, backward, left, and right directions. It can not move diagonally.





Give a Check

Check means that the opponent has attacked your king. You have to move out of the check on the very next move. Otherwise, you cannot continue the game.

You can try the following techniques to get out of the check.

    1. Move the king to a safe square where it gets out of the attack. 
    2. Capture the piece from which the king is getting checked. 
    3. Block the attack with one of your pieces.

If you are unable to remove the check using these techniques, you will lose the game.

Checkmate Opponent

Each player needs to checkmate the other to win the game, where the king gets captured, and the opponent has no move left to save the king.

You also have to support the piece with whom you are giving checks. It will be a plus point and benefit you.

Win the Game

Chess is the game of intelligence and strategy. You have to checkmate the opponent with a move that the opponent cannot foresee. It requires a proper technique with good thinking and intelligence.

There are many strategies to capture the king. You can apply Queen’s gambit, English opening, the four knights game, and other strategies to win a game. More strategies you master, the higher the chance to win.

Draw Conditions

The draw means that both players have agreed to tie. In tournaments, both players get 0.5 (1/2) points if there is a tie.

There are some conditions when the game can be declared a draw.

Stalemate

It happens when a player makes a move that is not giving a check, but the other player got no option left with either king or other pieces. It is one of the biggest reasons to get a match draw.

Mutual Agreement

Players can draw the match by mutual agreement. Such that a player can offer a draw to the opponent player at any time during the game. If the player accepts it, the game will be declared a draw, and each player gets awarded half points.

Threefold Repetition

If the same position occurs thrice, the game will be declared drawn. Such a situation is called threefold repetition.

For example, if players move the knight three times here and there, the game will be declared a draw. In modern chess, fivefold repetition is also considered the same way the threefold is.

50 Move Rule

If both players don’t move a pawn or do not capture any single piece for consecutive 50 moves, then you can claim a draw. In modern chess, the same rule is applied for 75 moves, called the 75 move rule.

Dead Position

When a situation occurs in which no player can give checkmate to the opponent with a valid move, the game will be a draw. Such a situation is called a dead position.

It usually occurs when both the players have no material left to checkmate the king. Such that there would be only two kings remaining on the board.

Conclusion

Chess is one of the most complex games around the world. But playing chess is not a difficult task. You just need to remember and follow the basic chess rules while keeping fully attentive during the game.

This game requires a deep focus and an active mind to become a better player. It also helps you to develop your brain and handle pressure in a difficult situation.

Learning and playing chess following the above steps is a lot of fun. You can play with a friend or a family member to effectively enjoy the gameplay. So get a chess set right now, and let’s start playing.
